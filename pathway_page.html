DIAGRAM 01 · Molecular Biology
DNA Replication Fork
The replication fork — helicase, primase, leading vs lagging strand synthesis, Okazaki fragments. Step through all 5 stages with annotations and a memory trick.
5 steps · Click-through
Step 1 of 5
SME Annotation
The Y-shape is the fork. Both new strands grow 5'→3' — but the templates run antiparallel. This forces the lagging strand to synthesise in the opposite direction to fork movement. This antiparallel constraint is the most commonly misunderstood concept in DNA replication.
Memory Trick
Think of Helicase as a zipper-opener running along a jacket zipper. SSB proteins are like paperclips holding each side open so it doesn't zip back up.
H = Helicase = "H"elps open the Helix
Memory Trick
Primers are like a running start in a race. DNA Pol cannot start from nothing — it needs someone to "fire the starting gun" (primase) and give it the first few steps (primer) before it can sprint.
P = Primer = "P"aves the way for Pol III
SME Annotation — Common AI Error to Watch For
Many AI models incorrectly state the lagging strand is synthesised 3'→5'. It is NOT. The lagging strand is synthesised 5'→3' in short segments that go in the opposite direction to the fork. This is a frequent factual error in AI-generated biology content.
Memory Trick — Full Sequence
Remember the full enzyme order as: "Helicase Starts, Primase Primes, Pol III Produces, Pol I Patches, Ligase Links"
H → P → III → I → L : "Happy People Produce In Life"
Why this matters for Biology SME work
In AI evaluation (Project 1), Q1 tested RNA Polymerase — which students and AI models frequently confuse with DNA Pol. The ability to distinguish between enzymes, their directionality, and their specific roles is exactly the kind of nuanced knowledge required to annotate AI errors correctly.

DIAGRAM 03 · Molecular Biology / Genetics
Lac Operon — Gene Regulation in Prokaryotes
The lac operon has 4 states based on glucose and lactose availability. Click each condition to see what happens to gene expression — the visual changes in real time.
4 states · Toggle
Select a condition to see lac operon regulation
The lac operon is controlled by two signals: repressor (lactose-sensitive) and CAP-cAMP (glucose-sensitive). Maximum expression requires lactose AND absence of glucose.
Memory Trick — Lac Operon Logic
Think of it like ordering food: You only order a big lactose meal when glucose (your favourite food) is not available. When glucose IS present, you ignore lactose — why bother? The cell thinks the same way.
No glucose + Lactose = MAXIMUM expression (the cell is hungry AND lactose is available)
SME Annotation — AI Frequently Errors Here
AI models often state that the lac operon is "ON when lactose is present" — this is INCOMPLETE. Maximum expression requires BOTH lactose AND absence of glucose (catabolite repression via CAP-cAMP). An evaluator must catch this partial answer and classify it as "Incomplete Reasoning."
