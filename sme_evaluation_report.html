# Question AI-Generated Answer Correct? Error Type SME Feedback — Yash Sahu

Evaluation Summary

Out of 10 AI-generated responses evaluated, 5 were correct, 1 was partially correct, and 4 were incorrect. The most common error types identified were conceptual confusion (mixing up similar structures/processes), factual errors (incorrect statements about biological mechanisms), and incomplete reasoning (correct but missing critical nuance).

Key findings:

  • Molecular Biology — AI confused transcription with replication (Q1) and incorrectly described the Shine-Dalgarno sequence as eukaryotic (Q6). Both are high-risk errors for AI training data.
  • Cell Biology — AI attributed rough ER functions to smooth ER (Q3) — a common but significant error in organelle function annotation.
  • Genetics — AI definition of frameshift mutations was incomplete; it missed the critical exception where insertions/deletions in multiples of 3 do not cause a frame shift (Q8).
  • Ecology & Biochemistry — AI performed well, with accurate answers on eutrophication, ATP, trophic cascades, and competitive inhibition.

This evaluation reflects the core competencies required for Biology SME roles in AI training and RLHF workflows: identifying factual errors, recognising incomplete reasoning, and providing structured expert-level feedback on AI-generated scientific content.
