Q: What is the significance of the Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase pump and its physiological role?
The Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase actively transports 3 Na⁺ out and 2 K⁺ in per ATP hydrolysed, maintaining resting membrane potential (~-70mV in neurons), regulating cell volume via osmotic pressure, and creating electrochemical gradients for secondary active transport. It is electrogenic and essential for restoring ion gradients post-action potential.
Cell BiologyHardActive TransportMembrane PotentialNeurophysiology
Q: What is the difference between competitive and non-competitive enzyme inhibition?
Competitive inhibition: inhibitor binds active site; Km↑, Vmax unchanged — overcome by excess substrate. Non-competitive: binds allosteric site; Vmax↓, Km unchanged — cannot be overcome. Example: Statins (competitive, HMG-CoA reductase); cyanide (non-competitive, cytochrome c oxidase).

RLHF Demonstration

How I Work as an
AI Evaluator

This is the exact workflow I followed in Project 1 — mapped to real RLHF pipeline stages.

01
Read question + AI response
Understand what the question is actually asking, then read the AI response without bias — treating it like a student answer to be graded.
Input: "What is the function of smooth ER?"
AI Answer: "Smooth ER is responsible for protein synthesis..."
02
Assess accuracy against domain knowledge
Cross-check each factual claim against known biology. Identify specific incorrect statements, missing nuance, or confused terminology.
Flag: SER has no ribosomes → cannot do protein synthesis
This is rough ER function → conceptual confusion
03
Classify verdict + error type
Assign Correct / Partial / Incorrect verdict. Classify error type: Factual Error, Conceptual Confusion, Incomplete Reasoning, or Multiple Errors.
Verdict: Incorrect
Error type: Conceptual confusion (SER ↔ RER)
04
Write structured SME feedback
Provide corrective explanation — precise, scientifically accurate, formatted for use in AI training data pipelines.
Feedback: "SER lacks ribosomes — not involved in protein synthesis. Actual functions: lipid synthesis, steroid hormone production, drug detoxification (liver), Ca²⁺ storage (muscle)."
